Information We Collect
RoadsideAmerica.com collects information from you when you use the website, sign up to receive our newsletter, voluntarily submit a roadside attraction tip, photo, video, feedback form or email, and provide us with your comments and other content.
Roadside America Newsletter: When you voluntarily sign up to receive our newsletter, you provide your email address to enable us to send you the newsletter. When you submit information through our contact form or post comments, you provide your name, email address, and any other information you decide to provide.
Roadside America Tip Submission: If you submit a new or updated attraction suggestion for inclusion in our collection of roadside attractions, you provide us with your email address (so we can verify the origin of the submission and contact you if it is published) and a username (so we can publish a credit for the tip). For your privacy protection we do not publish your email address without your consent. If you submit a tip accompanied by an explicit request to publish your email address, the decision to publish that information is solely at our discretion. Tip contributor contact information and source content may be retained in our internal company files only for as long as RoadsideAmerica.com determines it is necessary.
If you create an Account on RoadsideAmerica.com for the purposes of accessing special tools or content, you provide us with an email address and username.
Roadside America Mobile Apps: If you create an Account for the Roadside America iPhone app and make a region in-app purchase, the Apple App Store will verify the transaction. Apple does not give us access to your personal Apple data. Our apps are used under these End User License Agreements to which the user consented to before purchasing.
Ads: RoadsideAmerica.com displays ads that are provided by an advertising network. Google AdSense, in partnership with advertising companies, ad technology and ad verification services, may set cookies on your browser or read cookies that are already there. Google uses information to detect fraud, measure ad effectiveness, and personalize content. Google provides controls for you to see and control your information. Learn more: google.com/policies/technologies/partner-sites
Server Logging: RoadsideAmerica.com automatically receives and records information on our server logs from your browser. We may use information for the following general purposes: to customize the advertising and content, improve our services, conduct research, and provide anonymous reporting for internal and external clients. We and service providers acting on our behalf, such as Google Analytics, use Log Files and tracking technologies to collect and analyze certain types of technical information, including cookies, IP addresses, device type, device identifiers, browser types, browser language, referring and exit pages, and URLs, platform type, the number of clicks, domain names, landing pages, pages viewed and the order of those pages, the amount of time spent on particular pages, date and time of activity on our websites or applications, and other similar information. In some cases, we will associate this information with a user ID number for our internal use. End users can choose to opt out of Google Analytics .
