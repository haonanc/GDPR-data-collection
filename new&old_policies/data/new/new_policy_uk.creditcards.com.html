What information does UK CreditCards.com collect?

We do not require you to create an account or share any personally identifiable information in order to use our site. However, we do collect some non-personally identifiable information automatically. Further, solely at your option, you can choose to share some contact information with us.

Information Collected Automatically
UK CreditCards.com does NOT automatically collect personally identifiable information from your computer when you browse our site. We automatically collect some non-personally identifiable information about your visit, including the operating system and browser you are using, as well as the date, time, and pages you visit. Collecting this information helps us design the site to suit your needs.

When you request a page from our site, our servers log your current IP address. An Internet Protocol ("IP") address is a unique string of numbers that is automatically assigned to your computer by your Internet Service Provider. We use IP addresses to help diagnose problems and to gather demographic information and measure traffic to the site. We do not link IP addresses to any personally identifiable information about you.

UK CreditCards.com also allows third party partners to place session cookies on your computer to collect non-personally identifiable information such as aggregate use statistics for internal evaluation of the site. See below for additional information on cookies. Information You Provide to Us (Optionally)

We obtain personally identifiable information about you if you elect to send an inquiry to us through the 'contact us' section of our website, or subscribe to our newsletter or marketing emails. Please note that this information is only used to communicate with you regarding your inquiry or request for a newsletter (See Choice/Opt-Out for preference options). Does UK CreditCards.com use cookies to collect information about me?

Cookies are small files that are downloaded onto a user's computer when they visit a website.

There are a number of reasons why websites use cookies, including;

  • Enabling customers to use online "Shopping Basket" functionality (rather than having to make each purchase individually)
  • Improving the on-site user experience, by giving the site's webmasters visibility of online traffic through their site
  • Improving the targeting of online advertising, like banner adverts, so users are more likely to see adverts which are relevant to them
  • Allowing advertisers to pay online publishers on a performance basis

It is important to remember that cookies do not give webmasters access to your computer. We can only ever see information that you choose to share with us.

There are two types of cookies which are used: session based cookies and persistent cookies. Session based cookies are deleted from your computer as soon as you close your web browser. Persistent cookies are stored on your computer until they are deleted or they expire. Persistent cookies will have different expiry dates depending on its function. This is known as the 'lifetime' of the cookie.

Using your web browser settings you can choose whether to accept or decline cookies. As a rule most browsers are set to accept cookies as default but this can be manually modified. Doing this will, however, limit the ways you can use some sites and may prevent you from completing purchases.

Choice/Opt-out
We want our users to have the best possible experience on UK CreditCards.com. To ensure this and to allow for full use of our comparison services we advise that you enable the use of cookies. If cookies are not enabled it may limit your experience on the site and you may not be able to complete some purchases.

Enabling Cookies on Your Device
To enable cookies please follow the instructions on your preferred browser (please note that if you use more than one browser/device you will need to change to your desired settings on each one).

Accepting cookies is a condition of using UK CreditCards.com. Should you disable cookies we cannot guarantee or predict how our website will perform during your visit.

Third Party Cookies
Some of the cookies on UK CreditCards.com are set up on behalf of our third party suppliers. This is part of the process that allows us to offer you this comparison service.

We employ a third party (Adobe Omniture) to collect anonymous, non-personally identifiable information to evaluate the effectiveness of our websites. This vendor uses cookies and transparent images called "web beacons" to collect this information. Like cookies, web beacons do not allow us or the vendor to identify you personally. This data helps us understand how visitors use our website, such as the most visited pages and features. Omniture does not collect any personally identifiable information from you, and they do not share the non-personally identifiable information collected with anyone else for any other purpose. You may view their privacy policy here. We have no access or control over these types of third party cookies.

How does UK CreditCards.com secure the information collected?

UK CreditCards.com has security measures in place and takes reasonable precautions to protect against the loss, misuse, and unauthorised access of your personal information under our control. To help us accomplish this, we adopt the following procedures:

  • Periodically test our security procedures to ensure personnel and technical compliance.
